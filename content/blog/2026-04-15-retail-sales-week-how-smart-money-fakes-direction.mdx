## Step 2: Map the 48-Hour Liquidity Architecture Before the Release

The setup for a Retail Sales play actually begins **two days before the release**, not the morning of. Here's how I approach it:

**48 Hours Out — Identify the Pools:**
I drop down to the 4-hour chart on a primary USD pair — EURUSD, GBPUSD, or DXY if you're watching the dollar directly. I'm looking for:

- **Equal highs or equal lows** — these are liquidity magnets. Smart money knows retail traders cluster stops at these levels.
- **Recent swing highs/lows with obvious wicks** — these mark prior liquidity raids worth noting.
- **Unfilled Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)** — imbalances left behind from the previous session's institutional activity.

**24 Hours Out — Watch for the Raid:**
The day before Retail Sales, I expect — not hope for, *expect* — a liquidity sweep in the opposite direction of the anticipated move. If market consensus expects a strong number and bullish USD, I'm watching for a sweep of buy-side liquidity (a push above equal highs) followed by a sharp rejection. That rejection is your signal that smart money has been quietly distributing into retail long entries.

## Step 3: Identify the Pre-News Order Block

Once I've seen the liquidity raid occur — that sweep and rejection I mentioned — I'm hunting for the **order block** that sits at the origin of the move.

In **smart money concepts**, an order block is the last bearish candle before a strong bullish displacement (for a bullish OB), or the last bullish candle before a strong bearish displacement (for a bearish OB). This is where institutional orders were placed. Price tends to return to these zones to pair with remaining orders before continuing the higher-timeframe move.

Here's my specific process on Retail Sales week:

1. **Mark the sweep candle** — the candle that pushed into and through the liquidity pool.
2. **Identify the displacement** — the sharp move away from that sweep (usually 3-5 strong 15M candles moving in one direction).
3. **Find the origin OB** — the last candle in the opposing direction before the displacement began. This is your entry zone.
4. **Drop to 15M or 5M** — wait for price to retrace back into that OB zone *before the release*.
5. **Look for mitigation** — a wick into the OB, a small FVG fill, or a Judas swing into the zone confirms institutional interest.

This is where patience becomes the edge. You're not entering on the sweep. You're not entering on the displacement. You're waiting for price to come back and offer you an entry at institutional pricing — inside that order block — with the news catalyst as the fuel, not the trigger.

## Step 4: The News Candle Is the Exit Signal, Not the Entry

I've said this to every student who's come through my [coaching program](/coaching), and I'll say it again here: **the news candle is your exit signal, not your entry signal.**

If you've correctly identified the pre-news order block, entered inside the zone the day before or the morning of the release, set your stop below the order block's low (for longs) or above its high (for shorts), and sized appropriately — the actual data release is the moment smart money's orders get filled against the public's panic.

That 40-pip spike? That's your take profit triggering. Not your entry.

Here's what the actual execution looks like on a bullish USD setup:

- **T-48H:** Identify daily/4H sell-side liquidity pool (equal lows, prior swing low)
- **T-24H:** Price sweeps below that liquidity, raids sell-side stops, sharp rejection higher
- **T-12H to T-4H:** Price retraces back toward the last bearish OB before the displacement (your entry zone)
- **Entry:** Limit order placed inside the OB on the 15M chart
- **Stop:** Below the OB low or below the liquidity pool that was swept (2-5 pips buffer)
- **Target:** Buy-side liquidity above (equal highs, prior swing high, premium array)
- **News release:** Price accelerates toward your target — you're already in profit

## Step 5: What Kills This Setup (And How to Avoid It)

No strategy is bulletproof. Here are the scenarios where this framework fails and how to manage them:

**The double sweep:** Sometimes price sweeps sell-side liquidity the day before, rallies slightly, then sweeps it *again* the morning of the release — running stops on anyone who positioned from the first raid. This is why I always wait for **displacement confirmation** before entering. A single wick into a level doesn't confirm a raid. I want to see a strong, impulsive move away from that level on elevated volume before I consider the OB valid.

**Counter-narrative data:** The number comes out massively opposite to consensus. Smart money already positioned the other way and you had no prior signals. This is rare but real. This is why position sizing matters more than entry precision. If you're risking 1% per trade, a rogue data point doesn't blow your account — it's just a losing trade.

**Overlapping risk events:** Retail Sales dropping alongside FOMC minutes, another Fed speaker, or CPI in the same week creates noise that can invalidate clean pre-positioning.
