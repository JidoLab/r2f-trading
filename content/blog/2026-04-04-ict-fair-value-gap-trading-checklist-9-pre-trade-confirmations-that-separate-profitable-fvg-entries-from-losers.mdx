# ICT Fair Value Gap Trading Checklist: 9 Pre-Trade Confirmations That Separate Profitable FVG Entries From Losers

I'll be brutally honest with you. During my first three years trading ICT concepts, I blew through more accounts than I care to admit because I treated every Fair Value Gap (FVG) like a guaranteed money printer.

Every three-candle imbalance looked like an opportunity. Every gap screamed "trade me now!" And my account balance screamed back with red numbers that kept growing.

That's when I learned the hard truth: **not all Fair Value Gaps are created equal**. The difference between profitable traders and those who burn through funded accounts isn't just spotting FVGs—it's knowing which ones to ignore.

After 10+ years in the markets and passing multiple FTMO challenges, I've developed this **ICT fair value gap trading checklist** that's saved me from countless losing trades. Today, I'm sharing all nine confirmations I use before risking a single dollar on any FVG setup.

> **Key Takeaway:** Not every Fair Value Gap deserves a trade — use a structured pre-trade checklist of confirmations to filter high-probability FVG entries from random imbalances that will drain your account.

## What Makes a Fair Value Gap Worth Trading?

Before diving into the checklist, let's quickly define what we're working with. A Fair Value Gap occurs when there's an imbalance between buying and selling pressure, creating a "gap" in price action where one candle's body doesn't overlap with the candle two periods away.

But here's what most traders miss: **FVGs aren't signals—they're simply areas of inefficiency**. The market will eventually return to fill these gaps, but timing and context determine whether that move generates profit or pain.

The [CME Group's research on market microstructure](https://www.cmegroup.com) shows that price inefficiencies get filled roughly 70% of the time, but only 40% of those fills occur with enough momentum to create profitable swing trades.

That's where systematic validation comes in.

## The 9-Point ICT Fair Value Gap Trading Checklist

### ✅ 1. Market Structure Alignment

**What to check:** Is the FVG aligned with the current market structure bias?

If you're looking at a bullish FVG but the daily structure shows clear bearish market structure with lower highs and lower lows, you're swimming against the current. I learned this lesson the hard way when I took a long position on a beautiful 15-minute FVG, only to watch higher timeframe selling pressure steamroll my position.

**Action step:** Confirm your FVG direction matches at least two higher timeframes before proceeding.

### ✅ 2. Premium/Discount Validation

**What to check:** Is price currently in premium or discount relative to the daily/weekly range?

Taking bullish FVG entries when price is already at 80% of the daily range rarely works. You're essentially buying at retail prices hoping for more retail buyers above you. Smart money operates differently. Understanding how to [trade ICT premium/discount arrays during April's volatility squeeze](/trading-insights/2026-04-04-how-to-trade-ict-premium-discount-arrays-during-april-s-volatility-squeeze-before-summer-doldrums-hit) can sharpen how you apply this confirmation in live market conditions.

**Action step:** Use a 50% retracement of the current daily/weekly range to determine if you're buying discount or selling premium.

### ✅ 3. Liquidity Proximity Check

**What to check:** Where is the nearest liquidity pool in relation to your FVG?

Every profitable trade needs a target, and in ICT methodology, those targets are liquidity pools—equal highs, equal lows, or obvious stop-loss clusters. If your FVG is pointing toward empty space with no clear liquidity target within reasonable distance, skip it. If you've ever wondered whether a move into your FVG is a genuine smart money entry or a trap, the distinction covered in [ICT liquidity grab vs stop hunt: 8 questions every trader asks](/trading-insights/2026-04-04-ict-liquidity-grab-vs-stop-hunt-8-questions-every-trader-asks-and-my-brutal-honest-answers) is essential reading.

**Action step:** Identify at least 1:2 risk-reward potential to a clear liquidity pool before considering the trade.

### ✅ 4. Killzone Timing Alignment

**What to check:** Are you trading during an active ICT killzone?

The London Open (2:00-5:00 AM EST) and New York Open (8:30-11:00 AM EST) killzones provide the algorithmic activity needed to drive FVG reactions. Trading FVGs during dead hours often results in choppy, unpredictable price action.

**Action step:** Prioritize FVG entries that align with major killzone openings, especially when combined with high-impact news events.

### ✅ 5. Order Block Confirmation

**What to check:** Is there a supporting order block (OB) near your FVG?

Order blocks represent areas where institutional orders were placed. When an FVG forms near a respected order block, it often signals that smart money is still interested in that price level. This confluence significantly increases the probability of a successful reaction.

**Action step:** Look for FVGs that form within 10-20 pips of previously respected order blocks on your trading timeframe.

### ✅ 6. Volume Profile Analysis

**What to check:** What does volume tell you about this price level?

High-volume nodes often act as magnets for price, while low-volume areas (Volume Profile gaps) can accelerate price movement through your FVG. Understanding volume context helps predict whether your FVG will get a quick reaction or a slow grind.

**Action step:** Use TradingView's Volume Profile tool to identify if your FVG sits in a high-volume area (likely bounce) or low-volume area (likely continuation).

### ✅ 7. Multi-Timeframe FVG Stack

**What to check:** Are there FVGs on multiple timeframes pointing in the same direction?

When I see a 4-hour FVG aligned with a 1-hour FVG and a 15-minute FVG all pointing in the same direction, that's when I get excited. Multiple timeframe confirmation dramatically improves win rates because you're aligned with various institutional order flows.

**Action step:** Scan at least three timeframes (your trading TF and two higher) for FVG alignment before entering.

### ✅ 8. News Event Proximity

**What to check:** Are there high-impact news events within 4 hours of your planned entry?

I've seen perfect FVG setups get obliterated by unexpected NFP or CPI releases. While some traders thrive on news-driven volatility, FVG strategies work best in algorithmic market conditions, not during fundamental volatility spikes.

**Action step:** Check [Forex Factory's economic calendar](https://www.forexfactory.com) and avoid FVG entries 2 hours before and after red-folder news events.

### ✅ 9. Risk Management Alignment

**What to check:** Does this FVG fit within your daily/weekly risk parameters?

This might seem obvious, but it's where most traders fail. A perfect FVG setup means nothing if taking the trade violates your risk management rules or pushes you over your daily loss limit.

**Action step:** Calculate your position size before analyzing the setup. If the required stop loss exceeds your risk tolerance, walk away regardless of how "perfect" the setup looks.

## A Real-World FVG Validation Example

Last Tuesday during the London session, I spotted a beautiful bullish FVG on EURUSD 15-minute chart. Here's how I ran through my checklist:

**Market Structure:** ✅ Daily showing bullish structure  
**Premium/Discount:** ✅ Price at 30% of daily range (discount)  
**Liquidity:** ✅ Equal highs 45 pips above  
**Killzone:** ✅ London Open active  
**Order Block:** ✅ Respected OB 8 pips below FVG  
**Volume:** ✅ Low volume area suggesting continuation  
**Multi-TF:** ❌ 4-hour showing bearish FVG above  
**News:** ✅ No high-impact events for 6 hours  
**Risk:** ✅ 1% risk for 2.2% reward potential

The multi-timeframe conflict made me pause. That bearish 4-hour FVG could create resistance exactly where I needed continuation. I skipped the trade.

Price did initially move in my favor, hitting what would have been +40 pips, but then got rejected hard at that 4-hour FVG level, ultimately closing below my entry point. The checklist saved me from a losing trade disguised as a winner.

## Common FVG Trading Mistakes This Checklist Prevents

**Trading every gap:** Without systematic validation, you'll overtrade and kill your win rate.

**Ignoring higher timeframes:** 15-minute FVGs mean nothing if the 4-hour structure is against you.

**Poor timing:** FVGs during inactive market hours rarely provide clean reactions.

**No clear targets:** Trading toward empty space is gambling, not trading.

## How I Use This Checklist in Live Markets

I keep this checklist as a saved template in my trading journal. When I spot a potential FVG, I literally go through each point and mark it as confirmed (✅) or failed (❌). I need at least 7 out of 9 confirmations to consider a trade, and certain confirmations (market structure, liquidity, risk management) are non-negotiable.
