Order blocks are a cornerstone of ICT (Inner Circle Trader) methodology. They represent areas on the chart where institutional traders have placed significant orders, leaving behind a footprint that smart retail traders can use to their advantage.

## What Are Order Blocks?

An order block is the last candle before a strong move in the opposite direction. Think of it as the "launch pad" from which price rockets away. When large institutions accumulate or distribute positions, they leave these signatures on the chart.

There are two types:

- **Bullish Order Block**: The last bearish (down) candle before a strong bullish move up. This is where institutions were buying.
- **Bearish Order Block**: The last bullish (up) candle before a strong bearish move down. This is where institutions were selling.

## Why Do Order Blocks Matter?

Institutional traders don't fill their entire position at once — they can't without moving the market against themselves. Instead, they leave unfilled orders at these levels. When price returns to an order block, those unfilled orders often get activated, causing price to react.

This is why order blocks act as **high-probability support and resistance zones**.

## How to Identify Order Blocks

1. **Look for a strong displacement** — a sharp, impulsive move away from an area
2. **Find the last opposite candle** before that move began
3. **Mark the full range** of that candle (open to close, or high to low)
4. **Wait for price to return** to that zone for a potential entry

> The key is patience. Not every order block will hold, but when combined with other ICT concepts like Fair Value Gaps and market structure, they become incredibly powerful.

## Putting It Into Practice

The best way to learn order blocks is through deliberate practice. Start by marking them on historical charts in [TradingView](https://www.tradingview.com), then observe how price reacts when it returns to those zones.
