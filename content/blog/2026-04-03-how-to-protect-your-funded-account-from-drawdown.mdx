# How to Protect Your Funded Account from Drawdown

After 10+ years in the markets and helping countless traders secure funding, I've seen one pattern repeat itself over and over: talented traders who nail their challenges but then blow their funded accounts within weeks.

The harsh reality? **Getting funded is just the beginning.** The real challenge starts when you're trading with someone else's capital and need to protect it like your own.

Today, I'm sharing the exact risk management framework I use to help my students not just get funded, but keep their accounts for years. These aren't theoretical concepts – they're battle-tested strategies that work in real market conditions.

## Why Most Funded Traders Fail Within 30 Days

The statistics are sobering. Over 80% of funded traders lose their accounts within the first month. But it's rarely because they can't read charts or identify **smart money concepts**.

The problem is psychological and procedural. Once traders get funded, they often:

- Increase their risk per trade thinking "it's not my money"
- Abandon the conservative approach that got them funded
- Chase quick profits to reach payout thresholds faster
- Ignore their **daily drawdown limits** after a few winners

**Here's the mindset shift that changes everything:** Treat funded capital more carefully than your own money, not less. Your reputation and future funding opportunities depend on it.

## The ICT Risk Management Framework for Funded Accounts

### Position Sizing: The Foundation of Account Protection

In my [coaching plans](/coaching), the first thing I teach is proper position sizing using **ICT concepts**. Your position size should be calculated based on your **stop loss** distance to your **order block** or **fair value gap**.

Here's my proven formula:
- **Maximum risk per trade:** 0.5-1% of account balance
- **Daily risk limit:** 2% of account balance maximum
- **Weekly risk cap:** 5% of account balance

Let's say you have a $100,000 funded account. Your maximum risk per trade should be $500-$1,000, regardless of how "sure" you feel about a setup.

### Understanding Market Structure for Better Entries

**Market structure** analysis is crucial for protecting your account because it helps you identify high-probability setups with tight stop losses.

When I analyze market structure, I look for:
- **Break of structure (BOS)** for trend continuation trades
- **Change of character (CHOCH)** for potential reversals
- **Order blocks** that align with higher timeframe bias
- **Fair value gaps** that haven't been filled

The key insight? Better entries mean tighter stops, which means smaller position sizes and better risk-reward ratios.

### The Power of Confluence in Risk Reduction

Never take a trade based on a single ICT concept. I always look for **confluence** between multiple factors:

1. **Higher timeframe bias** aligning with my trade direction
2. **Premium or discount** pricing in the market
3. **Liquidity sweeps** that create favorable entry conditions
4. **Time-based analysis** using **ICT kill zones**

When you have multiple confluences, you can be more confident in your trades and often use tighter stop losses.

## Daily Drawdown Management Strategies

### The Three-Strike Rule

This is one of my most important rules for funded account protection. After three losing trades in a day, I stop trading completely.

Here's why this works:
- Prevents emotional revenge trading
- Keeps daily losses within acceptable limits
- Forces you to review what went wrong
- Preserves capital for tomorrow's opportunities

### The 50% Rule for Winning Days

On profitable days, once you've made 50% of your daily target, reduce your position size by half for subsequent trades. This locks in profits while still allowing for additional gains.

For example, if your daily target is $1,000 and you're up $500, cut your normal position size in half for the rest of the session.

## Advanced Risk Management Techniques

### Scaling Out of Positions

Don't treat every trade as all-or-nothing. I teach my students to scale out of positions at key levels:

- **First target:** Take 30% off at 1:1 risk-reward
- **Second target:** Take another 40% off at 1:2 risk-reward  
- **Final target:** Let 30% run to 1:3 or trail your stop

This approach ensures you're profitable even if the market doesn't reach your final target.

### The Power of Partial Losses

Sometimes the best risk management decision is taking a small loss before price hits your full stop loss. If market structure changes against your position or you see signs of institutional selling/buying opposite to your trade, don't be afraid to exit early.

A 0.3% loss is always better than a 1% loss.

## Weekly and Monthly Account Reviews

### Performance Metrics That Matter

Track these key metrics weekly:
- **Win rate percentage**
- **Average risk-reward ratio**
- **Maximum daily drawdown**
- **Longest losing streak**
- **Profit factor** (gross profit ÷ gross loss)

If any metric starts deteriorating, it's time to reduce position size or take a break to reassess.

### The Monthly Reset Protocol

At the end of each month, I review all trades with my students. We look for:
- Patterns in losing trades
- Times of day with poor performance
- Currency pairs or indices causing problems
- Emotional triggers that led to rule violations

This systematic review process helps identify and eliminate account-threatening behaviors before they become habits.

## Psychology: The Hidden Account Killer

### Managing Success and Failure

Paradoxically, success can be as dangerous as failure for funded traders. After a string of wins, overconfidence creeps in. After losses, desperation takes over.

Both states lead to the same outcome: **increased risk-taking and account blow-ups.**

The solution? Develop **mechanical consistency** in your approach. Your 20th trade should follow the exact same rules as your first trade, regardless of whether you're up or down.

### The Compound Effect of Small Consistent Gains

Remember, funded accounts aren't about hitting home runs. They're about consistent base hits that compound over time.

A trader making 1% per week consistently will double their account in less than 18 months. That's far more impressive to prop firms than someone who makes 20% one month and loses 15% the next.

## Creating Your Personal Risk Management Plan

Every successful funded trader needs a written risk management plan. Here's what yours should include:

### Pre-Market Routine
- [ ] Review higher timeframe market structure
- [ ] Identify key levels and potential trade setups  
- [ ] Set maximum risk for the day
- [ ] Review previous day's performance

### During Market Hours
- [ ] Stick to predetermined position sizes
- [ ] Never move stop losses against your position
- [ ] Take partial profits at predetermined levels
- [ ] Stop trading after daily loss limit is hit

### Post-Market Analysis
- [ ] Journal all trades with screenshots
- [ ] Calculate actual risk-reward ratios
- [ ] Note emotional state during each trade
- [ ] Plan improvements for tomorrow
